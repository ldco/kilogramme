## Федеральный закон от 27.07.2006 № 152-ФЗ "О персональных данных"

### Article 1 — Scope
Applies to all processing of personal data by any means (automated and non-automated) by any entity operating in Russia or processing data of Russian citizens.

### Article 2 — Purpose
Protection of rights and freedoms of individuals during processing of their personal data, including right to privacy, personal and family secrets.

### Article 3 — Definitions
- **Персональные данные (personal data):** Any information relating to an identified or identifiable individual
- **Оператор (operator/data controller):** Entity that organizes and carries out processing
- **Обработка (processing):** Any action with personal data (collection, storage, transfer, deletion, etc.)
- **Распространение (dissemination):** Disclosure to an indefinite circle of persons
- **Предоставление (provision):** Disclosure to specific persons or a defined circle
- **Блокирование (blocking):** Temporary suspension of processing
- **Уничтожение (destruction):** Irreversible deletion
- **Обезличивание (anonymization):** Actions making it impossible to identify the subject
- **Трансграничная передача (cross-border transfer):** Transfer to foreign state authorities, individuals, or entities

### Article 4 — Governing Legislation
- Constitution of the Russian Federation
- International treaties of the RF
- 152-FZ itself
- Other federal laws determining cases and specifics of processing

### Article 5 — Principles of Processing
1. Lawful and fair basis
2. Processing limited to specific, predefined, and lawful purposes
3. Data volume and content must correspond to declared purposes — no excessive collection
4. Data must be accurate, sufficient, and current (not excessive for stated purpose)
5. Storage no longer than required by purpose (must be destroyed when purpose achieved)
6. **Не допускается объединение баз данных (databases containing personal data processed for incompatible purposes must not be combined)**

### Article 6 — Conditions of Processing
Processing IS lawful when:
1. With **consent** of data subject (Article 9)
2. Required by international treaty or law
3. Justice administration / court enforcement
4. For state/municipal services
5. Contract performance (where subject is a party)
6. Protection of life, health, or vital interests (consent impossible to obtain)
7. For operator's or third parties' **legitimate interests** (provided rights not violated)
8. Professional activities (journalism, science, literature) — subject to rights protection
9. Statistical or research purposes (subject to anonymization)
10. Access by unlimited persons is **granted by the subject** or at his request
11. Data subject to publication or mandatory disclosure per federal law

### Article 7 — Confidentiality
Operators and persons who gained access to personal data must not disclose to third parties or disseminate without subject's consent, unless otherwise provided by federal law.

### Article 8 — Publicly Available Sources
Surname, name, patronymic, year and place of birth, address, subscriber number, profession info, and other personal data **reported by the subject** may be included in publicly available sources. Subject can revoke at any time. Data may be withdrawn at any time by request of the subject or by court/authorized body decision.

### Article 9 — Consent (CRITICAL)
- Consent must be **specific, informed, and conscious** (конкретное, информированное и сознательное)
- Consent may be in **any form confirming receipt**: written, electronic, or implicit actions
- Operator must provide proof of consent
- Subject may **withdraw consent** at any time
- Processing may continue without consent for Article 6(2-11) cases
- Processing of **special categories** requires WRITTEN consent (Article 10)
- Consent for processing **publicly distributed** data given separately from other consent
- Requirements for consent form established by Roskomnadzor
- **Electronic consent** must be signed with electronic signature or other legally significant action
- **Consent revocation:** operator must stop processing and destroy data within 30 days unless otherwise specified (contract obligations, etc.)

### Article 10 — Special Categories of Personal Data
Special categories (race, nationality, political views, religious/philosophical beliefs, health, intimate life, criminal record) — processing NOT allowed except:
1. Written consent of subject
2. Publicly available data per the subject
3. Employment/labor law compliance
4. Protection of life/health (consent impossible)
5. Public association activities for statutory purposes
6. Medical purposes (by medical professionals with confidentiality obligations)
7. Insurance legislation
8. Archive/civil registry per law
9. Criminal proceedings per legislation
10. State social assistance/labor legislation

**Biometric data** — used only for identifying a person. Processing allowed only with WRITTEN consent except court enforcement, criminal investigation, defense/security, counter-terrorism, transport security.

### Article 11 — Biometric Personal Data
Data characterizing physiological and biological features of a person, used by operator for identity establishment. Processing only with WRITTEN consent. Exceptions: cases provided by Russian legislation on defense, security, counter-terrorism, transport safety, anti-corruption, operational-search activities, public prosecution, criminal enforcement, migration legislation.

### Article 12 — Cross-Border Transfer
Transfer of personal data to foreign states is allowed subject to ensuring adequate protection. Before cross-border transfer:
1. Operator must ensure the foreign state provides **adequate protection** of personal data subjects' rights
2. Transfer to states without adequate protection allowed only with **WRITTEN consent** of subject (execution of contract with subject, protection of life/health, or international treaty)
3. Roskomnadzor maintains **list of foreign states** ensuring adequate protection (white-list countries)

**Cross-border transfer notification:** From March 1, 2023, operators must notify Roskomnadzor of their intent to transfer personal data abroad before beginning such transfer.

### Article 13 — Local Acts on Personal Data Processing
Operators may adopt internal policies and local acts on processing defining:
- Processing purposes and procedure
- Data categories
- Protection measures
- Employee access rules
Must be published/available to employees.

### Article 14 — Data Subject Rights (CRITICAL for DSAR)
Data subject has the right:
1. To **obtain information** concerning processing of their personal data:
   - Confirmation of processing fact
   - Legal grounds and purposes
   - Methods of processing
   - Operator details (name, location)
   - Processed data categories and specific data
   - Persons who have access (including employees)
   - Processing periods, including storage periods
   - Information about completed or proposed cross-border transfers
   - Source of data (if not from subject)
   - Legal consequences of processing
2. To **demand rectification, blocking, or destruction** of data if it is:
   - Incomplete, outdated, inaccurate
   - Illegally obtained
   - Not necessary for the stated processing purpose
3. To receive data about themselves and source (unless federal law restriction)
4. To be informed about **legal consequences** of processing their data

**Response deadline:** Operator must respond within **10 working days** (or **30 calendar days** for blocking/destruction). Refusal must be **reasoned in writing**.

**Repeated request:** Permitted no earlier than 30 days from previous request.

### Article 15 — Rights to Rectification, Blocking, Destruction
Same as Article 14(2) — subject can demand:
- Rectification of inaccurate data
- Blocking of processing until verification
- Destruction if data is excessive, outdated, or illegally obtained
Must notify third parties who received this data, UNLESS impossible or disproportionate.

### Article 16 — Rights to Object to Automated Decision-Making
Decisions producing legal effects or significantly affecting the data subject cannot be based solely on **automated processing** of personal data. Exceptions: written consent OR provided by federal law (with safeguards for the subject's legitimate interests, including right to human review).

### Article 17 — Right to Appeal
Subject may appeal operator's actions/omissions to Roskomnadzor or in court. Moral damages compensated regardless of property damage.

### Article 18 — Operator Obligations
When collecting personal data:
1. Provide subject (on request) with information specified in Article 14
2. If data NOT received from subject, notify the subject (exceptions: already notified, data processed for statistics/research, confidentiality obligations, federal law exception)
3. Take measures to clarify incomplete/inaccurate data
4. Satisfy subject's lawful demands for clarification, blocking, destruction
5. **Notify Roskomnadzor** of processing commencement (Article 22)

### Article 18.1 — Data Protection Measures
Operators MUST implement:
1. Threat identification and classification
2. Organizational and technical measures per security levels
3. Security evaluation
4. Recording of machine-readable media
5. Detection of unauthorized access + prevention
6. Recovery of modified/destroyed data due to unauthorized access
7. Access control rules
8. Registration and accounting of all actions with personal data
9. **Proof** that required measures are in place (audit trail)

### Article 19 — Organizational and Technical Security Measures
- Appoint a **person responsible** for personal data processing
- Classification of information systems (4 levels: high/medium/low threats)
- Use of certified information protection means (ФСТЭК / FSTEC certification)
- Physical security: access control to premises, locks, safes for paper records
- Employee training on data protection

### Article 21 — Data Localization (since September 1, 2015)
**Critical provision:** During collection of personal data (including via the Internet), the operator must ensure **recording, systematization, accumulation, storage, clarification (updating, modification), and retrieval** of personal data of citizens of the Russian Federation using **databases located on the territory of the Russian Federation**.

This means: primary database must be in Russia. Cross-border transfer is still possible AFTER data is first stored in RF-based databases, under Article 12 conditions.

**Exception:** Processing for Article 6(1)(2,3,4,8,11) purposes (international treaty, justice, state services, journalism).

### Article 22 — Notification to Roskomnadzor (CRITICAL)
Before commencing processing, operators must notify Roskomnadzor. Notification must include:
1. Operator name, address, contact details
2. Purpose of processing
3. Categories of personal data
4. Categories of subjects whose data is processed
5. Legal basis of processing (consent, law, contract, etc.)
6. List of actions with personal data + processing methods
7. Description of protection measures
8. Name of responsible person (DPO equivalent)
9. Date of processing commencement
10. Term or condition of processing termination
11. Information on cross-border transfer (if applicable)
12. Information on database location (RF territory confirmation)

**Exceptions from notification (Article 22(2)):**
- Processed under labor legislation
- Received by operator solely for contract execution with subject and NOT disseminated or transferred
- Processed for single-pass access to operator's premises (security)
- Publicly available personal data
- Only surname, name, patronymic data
- Data necessary for single-issue pass to operator's territory or similar
- Religious/public organizations processing members' data for statutory purposes

### Article 22.1 — Person Responsible for Personal Data Processing
Operator must appoint person responsible for personal data processing (DPO equivalent). Responsibilities:
- Internal control over 152-FZ compliance
- Employee awareness of 152-FZ requirements
- Handling subject inquiries and requests

### Article 23 — Roskomnadzor Powers
Roskomnadzor as authorized body for protection of personal data subjects' rights:
- Maintains operator register
- Investigates complaints
- Issues binding orders to remedy violations
- May demand blocking, clarification, or destruction of data
- May bring administrative/criminal proceedings
- Sues in court for data subjects (including class actions)
- Cooperates with foreign DPAs on cross-border issues

### Article 24 — Liability
Violations → civil, criminal, administrative, or disciplinary liability per RF legislation.
Administrative fines (КоАП РФ Article 13.11):
- Processing without consent or improper consent: up to 150K RUB for companies
- Failure to publish processing policy: up to 60K RUB
- Failure to provide subject information: up to 80K RUB
- Failure to comply with destruction/blocking/deletion demands: up to 90K RUB
- Data localization violation: up to 18M RUB (for first offense)

## GDPR ↔ 152-ФЗ Mapping

| GDPR Article | 152-ФЗ Equivalent | Status |
|-------------|-------------------|--------|
| Art 5 — Principles | Art 5 | Similar: lawful, limited purpose, data minimization |
| Art 6 — Lawful basis | Art 6 | Similar but different list of bases |
| Art 7 — Consent conditions | Art 9 | Both require specific, informed, freely given consent |
| Art 8 — Child's consent | — | 152-ФЗ does NOT specify minimum age |
| Art 9 — Special categories | Art 10 | Both restrict processing; 152-ФЗ requires written consent |
| Art 12-14 — Transparency | Art 14, 18 | Both require informing subjects |
| Art 15 — Right of access | Art 14 | Both grant subject access right |
| Art 16 — Right to rectification | Art 14, 15 | Both grant rectification right |
| Art 17 — Right to erasure | Art 14, 15 | Both grant erasure right |
| Art 18 — Right to restriction | Art 15 | Both grant blocking of processing |
| Art 20 — Data portability | — | 152-ФЗ has NO explicit portability right |
| Art 21 — Right to object | Art 15(1) | 152-ФЗ has narrower objection right |
| Art 22 — Automated decisions | Art 16 | Both restrict automated decisions |
| Art 25 — Data protection by design | Art 19 | 152-ФЗ requires security measures but not explicit "by design" |
| Art 28 — Processor | — | 152-ФЗ uses "лицо, осуществляющее обработку по поручению" (Article 6(3)) |
| Art 30 — Records of processing | Art 22 | Both require notification/registration |
| Art 32 — Security of processing | Art 19 | Both require technical/organizational measures |
| Art 33-34 — Breach notification | — | 152-ФЗ has NO mandatory breach notification to authority |
| Art 35 — DPIA | Art 18.1 | 152-ФЗ requires threat analysis but not formal DPIA |
| Art 37 — DPO | Art 22.1 | Both require responsible person (DPO) |
| Art 44-49 — International transfers | Art 12 | Both regulate cross-border transfers |
| Art 50 — Data localization | Art 21 | 152-ФЗ is far MORE restrictive (must store on RF servers first) |
| — | — | No "legitimate interest" as GDPR defines it (152-ФЗ uses different list) |

## Cookie Law Requirements in Russia

### Legal Basis
- Article 9 of 152-ФЗ — consent must be specific, informed, conscious
- Article 18.1 — obligation to publish a personal data processing policy
- No specific "cookie law" like EU ePrivacy Directive

### Practical Requirements
1. **Cookie notification banner** — inform users what cookies are used and for what purpose
2. **Consent mechanism** — must be affirmative action (not pre-checked boxes)
3. **Policy document** — privacy policy must include cookie usage section
4. **Cookie audit** — document all cookies used (name, purpose, duration, category)
5. **Essential cookies** — may not require consent IF technically necessary for site function
6. **Analytics/marketing cookies** — REQUIRE consent before setting
7. **Consent evidence** — operator must be able to prove consent was given

### Practical Recommendations for PM Sites in Russia
- Binary consent (accept/decline) is acceptable but **granular** is better
- Must link to full privacy policy from consent banner
- Consent stored for at least duration of processing
- Cookie declaration page listing all cookies
- "Learn more" link from consent banner to cookie section of privacy policy

## Roskomnadzor Compliance Checklist for PM Websites

### Before Launch
- [ ] Personal data processing policy document published on site (Article 18.1)
- [ ] Cookie consent banner implemented
- [ ] Consent mechanism with proof storage
- [ ] Roskomnadzor notification submitted (Article 22) — unless exception applies
- [ ] Databases located on RF territory (Article 21)
- [ ] Person responsible for data processing appointed (Article 22.1)
- [ ] Internal data protection measures documented (Article 19)
- [ ] Data processing purposes clearly defined and limited
- [ ] Personal data categories documented
- [ ] Processing procedures documented
- [ ] Cross-border transfer procedures documented (if applicable)
- [ ] Third-party data sharing documented (with names of recipients)

### Ongoing Compliance
- [ ] DSAR response procedure (10 working days)
- [ ] Consent withdrawal processing (within 30 days)
- [ ] Data rectification/destruction procedure
- [ ] Annual review of data processing policy
- [ ] Employee training records
- [ ] Audit log of all data processing operations
- [ ] Security incident response plan
- [ ] Regular threat analysis updates

### Contact Form Specifics
- Privacy policy link MUST be near the submit button
- Checkbox for consent (not pre-checked)
- Consent text must specify: what data is collected, for what purpose, how long stored, who has access
- If using third-party email gateways — must disclose in policy
- If storing form data in database — must confirm RF territory storage

### Admin Panel Specifics
- Admin user data (emails, names, roles, login history) is personal data
- Must be included in processing policy
- Audit logs of admin actions support Article 18.1 proof requirements
- Access to user data must be role-limited and logged

## Cross-Border Transfer White List Countries (Roskomnadzor)

States ensuring adequate protection:
- Council of Europe Convention 108 signatories (most of Europe)
- Countries with recognized data protection laws

States NOT ensuring adequate protection:
- USA (no federal privacy law equivalent to GDPR/152-FZ)
- China (limited privacy protection)
- Most of Africa, Middle East, parts of Asia

Transfer to non-white-list countries: requires WRITTEN consent from data subject.

## Template: Personal Data Processing Policy Sections (152-FZ Compliant)

1. **Общие положения (General Provisions)**

## Template: Privacy Policy Cookie Section (Russian)

```
Обработка cookie-файлов

На сайте используются cookie-файлы — небольшие текстовые файлы, сохраняемые
в браузере пользователя. Cookie-файлы не содержат персональных данных
и используются исключительно в технических целях:

- Технические cookie (pm-session) — необходимы для функционирования системы
  авторизации и защиты от CSRF-атак. Срок хранения: 24 часа (сессия).

- Функциональные cookie (pm-cookie-consent, pm-color-mode, pm-locale-explicit) —
  сохраняют выбор пользователя в отношении использования cookie-файлов,
  цветовой схемы и языка интерфейса. Срок хранения: 1 год.

Пользователь может отказаться от использования cookie-файлов, выбрав
соответствующую опцию в баннере при первом посещении сайта, а также
в любой момент очистив cookie-файлы в настройках браузера.
```
